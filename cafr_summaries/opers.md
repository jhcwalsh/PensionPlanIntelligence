## Investment Policy Text
```
OPERS manages three distinct sub-portfolios—Defined Benefit, Health Care, and Defined Contribution—each governed by its own Statement of Investment Objectives and Policies approved by the Board of Trustees.

**Defined Benefit Portfolio**
Primary objective: secure statutory benefits and keep costs reasonable for members and employers. Performance objectives: (1) exceed the composite policy benchmark net of investment expenses over five-year periods, and (2) exceed the actuarial assumed rate of return of 6.9% over the long term. The 2024 long-term expected rate of return (calculated using 20-year projections, a methodology change introduced by new consultant Meketa) is 8.64%. Asset allocation targets: Public Equity 41% (range 33–49%), Fixed Income 24% (range 17–31%), Alternatives 33% (range 23–43%), Risk Parity 2% (range 0–5%), Operating Cash 0% (range 0–5%). Within Fixed Income, sub-allocations include Core Fixed 9%, Investment Grade Credit 3%, Emerging Markets Debt 1%, Securitized Debt 1%, TIPS 3%, High Yield 4%, U.S. Treasury 3%. Within Alternatives: Private Equity 15%, Real Estate 12%, Private Credit 1%, REITs 1%, Opportunistic 2%, Commodities 2%. The portfolio is managed using both active and passive strategies, internally and externally. In November 2024, the Board approved creation of a dedicated Risk Mitigation Strategies composite to enhance portfolio resilience with defensive characteristics and low equity-market correlation; implementation is planned for 2025–2026.

**Health Care Portfolio**
Primary objectives: preservation of capital, earning a reasonable return, and liquidity. The Health Care portfolio is invested with a more liquid, shorter-term asset-allocation strategy than the Defined Benefit portfolio. Actuarial assumed rate of return: 6.0%. Asset allocation targets: Public Equity 50% (range 40–60%), Fixed Income 37% (range 26–48%), Alternatives 10% (range 7–20%), Risk Parity 3% (range 0–5%), Operating Cash 0% (range 0–5%). Fixed Income sub-allocations: Core Fixed 16%, Investment Grade Credit 3%, Emerging Markets Debt 1%, Securitized Debt 3%, TIPS 7%, High Yield 5%, U.S. Treasury 2%. Alternatives sub-allocations: REITs 5%, Opportunistic 2%, Commodities 3%. No private equity or private real estate; no private credit allocation.

**Rebalancing and Risk Management**
Portfolios are reviewed daily for compliance with target asset allocation percentages. A comprehensive strategic asset allocation review is completed approximately every three-to-five years or if market conditions change substantially; the most recent review was completed in 2024 for 2025 allocations. The Board ensures risk control through diversification, portfolio guidelines, risk budgeting, compliance, and ongoing monitoring. In April 2024, the Board hired Meketa as its new investment consultant, which introduced a new functional allocation framework to improve portfolio transparency, oversight, and risk assessment.

**Derivatives and Leverage**
Derivatives are used to mitigate risk and improve trading efficiency. The combined gross notional exposure of Category II and III derivatives will not exceed 50% of total net asset value of public market assets held in separate accounts (excluding FX hedging). Currency forwards are limited to one year or less to maturity unless approved by the CIO.

**Securities Lending**
OPERS operates an active securities lending program. Excess collateral is marked to market daily at a minimum of 102% for domestic securities and 105% for international securities. Maximum assets on loan: 50% of eligible assets; maximum with any single borrower: 15% of eligible assets.

**Public Equities**
Both active and passive strategies are employed, tracked using tracking error ranges: U.S. Equity 0–100 bps vs. Russell 3000; Non-U.S. Equity 0–300 bps vs. custom benchmark.

**Private Equity**
Exclusively external, active managers. Single closed-end commingled fund commitment limited to $600M for primary funds, $800M for fund-of-funds. Benchmark: State Street Private Equity Index (SSPEI).

**Private Real Estate**
Active management via external managers. Non-U.S. holdings limited to 25% of real estate NAV. Single property limit: $500M; single closed-end fund: $750M; single open-end fund: $1B. Benchmark: Net NFI-ODCE plus 85 bps.

**Divestment Policies**
Investment-wide policies include Iran and Sudan Divestment Policy and Russia Divestment Policy. An Ohio-Qualified and Minority-Owned Manager Policy is also in place.
```
