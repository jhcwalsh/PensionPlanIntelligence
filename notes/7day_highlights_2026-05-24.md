## Manager Hires, Terminations, and Mandate Changes

**KPPA** (~$20B, KY) CERS Investment Committee unanimously approved transferring its All Country World Ex US equity mandate from **Franklin Templeton** to **ClearBridge** (a Franklin Templeton affiliate), with **ClearBridge** offering zero management fees through June 30, 2027 to cover transition costs ([source](?doc=4289)). The committee cited sustained underperformance, loss of significant clients, and internal staff moves at Franklin Templeton as the basis for the change ([source](?doc=4289)). The committee also unanimously recommended a **$90 million** commitment to **Capitol Meridian Fund II**, targeting aerospace & defense, government technology, and government services middle-market businesses in North America, contingent on successful contract negotiations ([source](?doc=4289)). An additional commitment to **ITE Rail Fund** brought total KPPA exposure to **$500 million**; the fund is essentially closed to new investors but KPPA was invited due to its existing relationship ([source](?doc=4289)).

**LAFPP** (~$29B, CA) is moving to terminate **AllianceBernstein L.P.**, **Channing Capital Management LLC**, and **Westwood Management Corp** from small cap value equity, and to eliminate Smart Beta strategies including **Northern Trust STOXX USA 900 Min Variance** and **AllianceBernstein U.S. Systematic Value** (combined **$1,606 million**) ([source](?doc=4299)). A search for a new active small cap value equity manager is underway, with an anticipated mandate size of **$150–250 million**; finalists are expected in 3Q26 with interviews in early 4Q26 ([source](?doc=4299)). The restructuring is projected to reduce domestic equity management fees from **9 basis points** to **7 basis points**, saving approximately **$2.8 million** annually ([source](?doc=4299)).

**PERSI** (~$26B, ID) unanimously approved hiring **Hood River Capital Management** and **Boston Partners** as investment managers ([source](?doc=4278)).
