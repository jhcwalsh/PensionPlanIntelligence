## Risk Mitigation Portfolio Performance: LACERA's RRM Outperforms

LACERA's Credit and Risk Mitigation Committee reviewed the **$22.26 billion** Risk Reduction and Mitigation (RRM) portfolio, which carries a 24% total fund target allocation — up from 19% following the 2024 Strategic Asset Allocation revision. The portfolio outperformed its policy benchmark across all measured periods: **6.8% vs. 6.0%** (1-year), **5.1% vs. 5.0%** (3-year), and **1.5% vs. 1.1%** (5-year), all as of February 28, 2026.

Component-level detail is instructive. **Investment Grade Bonds** ($11.723B, 70% passive/30% active) returned **6.4% vs. 6.3%** benchmark over one year, with effective duration of 5.8 years and YTW of 4.2%. **Long-Term Government Bonds** ($1.716B) matched benchmark at **3.7%** but carry a 13.9-year duration and a deeply negative inception-to-date return of **-6.4% annualized** since November 2021 — a persistent mark of the 2022 rate shock. **Hedge Funds** ($7.544B) are benchmarked to T-Bills + 200 bps following a July 2024 reset from T-Bills + 250 bps, a modest reduction in the performance hurdle. The RRM portfolio provides **37 months** of benefit payment liquidity, excluding hedge funds — a meaningful buffer in volatile markets.
