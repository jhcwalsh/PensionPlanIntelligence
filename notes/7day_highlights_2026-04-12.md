## Performance Data: OPERF Underperformance Warrants Attention

## Portfolio Strategy: CalPERS Reshapes Incentive Architecture Around TPA

**CalPERS** (**$502B**, CA) used its April 13–14 board sessions and a prior April 2 education session with advisor **Global Governance Advisors** (Peter Landers and Brad Kelly) to advance a fundamental overhaul of its incentive compensation framework tied to the transition to a **Total Portfolio Approach** beginning FY2026-27. The new structure measures performance against a simple **75/25 Global Equity/Global Bond Reference Portfolio**, replacing the prior 11-benchmark customized SAA model.

Key structural changes under review: a five-year phased blending of TPA and legacy SAA results for incentive calculations; introduction of a 10% Collaboration metric for investment staff below senior leadership; and addition of a **15% Total Fund performance weighting** for the COO, CFO, and General Counsel—reducing organizational silos. The Chief Actuary is explicitly excluded from fund performance metrics due to conflict-of-interest concerns. The FY2026-27 incentive budget stands at **$43.5 million** for annual awards and **$13.6 million** for long-term awards, against FY2024-25 actuals of **$29.9 million** and **$8.8 million** respectively. Final metric approval deferred to June 2026.

VRS (**$110B**, VA) Investment Advisory Committee discussed possible benchmark changes and asset allocation adjustments—including a foreign adversaries update—with formal recommendations forwarded to the Board of Trustees.
