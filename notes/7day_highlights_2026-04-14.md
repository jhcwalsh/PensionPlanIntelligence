## Risk Portfolio Performance

LACERA's Credit and Risk Mitigation Committee reviewed its Risk Reduction and Mitigation (RRM) portfolio, which was expanded from a 19% to **24%** total fund target allocation in the 2024 Strategic Asset Allocation. The portfolio's market value stood at **$22.26 billion** as of February 28, 2026, allocated across **Investment Grade Bonds** (**$11.72B**, 13% target), **Hedge Funds** (**$7.54B**, 8% target), **Long-Term Government Bonds** (**$1.72B**, 2% target), and **Cash** (**$1.28B**, 1% target).

Performance across the RRM composite outpaced benchmarks at every measured horizon: **6.8% vs. 6.0%** (1-year), **5.1% vs. 5.0%** (3-year), and **1.5% vs. 1.1%** (5-year) through February 2026. Investment Grade Bonds — structured **70% passive / 30% active** with effective duration of 5.8 years and YTW of **4.2%** — added incremental alpha at each time period. The Long-Term Government bond sleeve (duration 13.9 years, YTW 4.6%) matched its benchmark but carries a notable inception-to-date drag of **-6.4% annualized** since November 2021, a direct consequence of the 2022 rate spike. The hedge fund benchmark was reset in July 2024 from T-Bills +250 bps to **T-Bills +200 bps**. In aggregate, the RRM portfolio provides **37 months** of benefit payment liquidity, excluding hedge funds.
