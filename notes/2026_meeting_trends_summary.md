# 2026 Meeting Agenda Trends: Key Topics & Announcements

*Covering January–April 2026 across 18 major U.S. pension plans (~$1.9 trillion AUM)*

---

## **Private Markets Deployment: Commitments Accelerating Across Buyout, Credit, and Venture**

Private markets activity has been the most consequential investment story across 2026 meeting agendas. Boards and investment committees are deploying capital across buyout, venture, credit, and secondaries at a pace that signals sustained conviction despite elevated macro uncertainty.

LACERA ($75B) led the most detailed public disclosure, with the CIO approving $416 million in private equity commitments through the March 11, 2026 board cycle: a $350 million commitment to AE Industrial Partners IV (industrials/materials buyout, North America-focused), a $41 million co-investment alongside Kingswood Capital Management, and a $25 million co-investment alongside Primary Venture Partners. The April 8 Board of Investments meeting separately approved a one-year extension to LACERA's private equity consulting agreement with StepStone Group LP, along with selection of a new fiduciary counsel panel.

The Washington State Investment Board ($200B) convened its Private Markets Committee on April 2, 2026 to consider three separate fund commitments: Charterhouse Capital Partners XII (pan-European mid-market buyout, targeting €1.5–2.0bn, focused on Services and Healthcare), Spark Capital IX ($770M venture fund) and Spark Capital Growth VI ($1.5B venture growth fund) — both from a firm with $8.7B committed AUM across 13 funds — and Monarch Capital Partners VII (private credit/distressed, under WSIB's Innovation Portfolio sleeve). Charterhouse's predecessor performance was cited in diligence materials: CCP X generated a 20% net IRR and 2.0x TVPI, while CCP XI has so far produced a 15% net IRR and 1.3x TVPI. The committee also held an education session specifically on private credit benchmarking.

Minnesota SBI ($161B) ratified eleven private markets commitments originally approved at the October 2025 meeting, totaling up to $1.975 billion. Named commitments include up to $150 million to Advent International GPE XI SCSP, up to $300 million to Blackstone Supplemental Account–M, and up to $250 million to Strategic Partners X (Blackstone's secondaries vehicle).

The Dallas Police & Fire Pension System ($3B) committed $35 million to Blackstone Strategic Partners X (private equity secondaries) and up to $15 million combined to Lexington Continuation Vehicle Investors and a related co-investment vehicle, both approved at the March 12, 2026 board meeting and confirmed in the April 2 agenda materials. DPFP also expanded Albourne's advisory mandate to cover Private Equity, Private Credit, and Real Assets for an additional $25,000 in annual fees.

The Houston Police Officers' Pension System ($8B) placed three PE fund commitments on its April 7, 2026 Investment Committee agenda for Board recommendation: Ares Pathfinder III, Kingswood Capital Opportunities Fund IV, and LS Power Equity Partners VI — spanning credit/opportunistic, lower middle-market buyout, and energy infrastructure strategies respectively. A real estate transaction at 1600 Smith was simultaneously flagged for Board recommendation.

## **Manager Hires and Mandate Changes: Specialty Equity Mandates and Alternatives Infrastructure**

Manager selection activity in 2026 reflects a deliberate rotation toward specialty active mandates in equities and a build-out of alternatives advisory infrastructure at multiple funds.

IPERS ($41B) hired three managers for its absolute return/portable alpha program, all approved at the December 5, 2025 Investment Board meeting and reflected in the March 27, 2026 board pack: Florin Court Capital (Florin Court Capital Programme), Capital Fund Management SA (CFM ISTEC Program), and AHL Partners LLP (AHL Cat Bond Program). These three appointments collectively represent a diversified liquid alternatives overlay designed to enhance risk-adjusted returns without stretching illiquidity. IPERS' 2025 total fund return of 13.75% vs. a 13.40% policy benchmark provided the performance backdrop for this expansion.

LACERS ($25B) approved one-year contracts with Granahan Investment Management for an active U.S. Small Cap Growth Equities portfolio and Copeland Capital Management for an active U.S. Small Cap Core Equities portfolio at its March 24, 2026 Board of Administration meeting. In parallel, the board continued discussion on a private credit consulting contract with Aksia LLC — a signal that the fund is formalizing advisory infrastructure before expanding its private credit allocation.

LACERA advanced a Passive Cash Overlay Investment Manager Search to the contract development stage, with five finalists identified: Legal & General Asset Management, NISA Investment Advisors, Parametric Portfolio Associates, Russell Investments, and State Street Investment Management. This search reflects a growing trend among large public funds toward overlay programs designed to manage cash drag and currency hedging efficiently.

The Mississippi Public Employees' Retirement System ($29B) held information-only reviews of four global public equity managers — PGIM Quant, Harding Loevner, Acadian, and LSV Global Value — with Callan providing market and performance context at the April 21 Investment Committee meeting. No actions were taken, suggesting the fund is in active monitoring mode rather than re-tendering.

## **Portfolio Strategy Shifts: TPA Adoption, Allocation Reviews, and Benchmark Restructuring**

Several large plans are executing foundational strategy reviews that will reshape portfolio construction over the next several years.

CalPERS ($502B) is formally transitioning to a Total Portfolio Approach (TPA) beginning FY2026-2027. Multiple Performance, Compensation & Talent Management Committee meetings in April 2026 — on April 2 and April 13 — centered on the incentive compensation implications of TPA, specifically how to integrate TPA investment performance versus the new Reference Portfolio into the staff incentive formula. Global Governance Advisors (GGA) recommended a five-year phased blend of legacy Strategic Asset Allocation and new TPA performance results, benchmarking CalPERS' design against the Australian Future Fund, New Zealand Superannuation Fund, and CPP Investments. The 2026-27 budget includes $43.5 million earmarked for annual incentive awards across executive and investment management roles. Final GGA recommendations are expected in June 2026.

The Virginia Retirement System ($110B) convened its Investment Advisory Committee on April 8, 2026, to address possible benchmark changes, portfolio diversification updates, and a public markets program review — with formal recommendations to be transmitted to the Board of Trustees on benchmark modifications and asset allocation adjustments. Specific parameters were not disclosed in public agenda materials.

TMRS ($40B) reported exceptional 2025 total fund performance of 13.29%, outperforming its benchmark by 107 basis points and generating $5.6 billion in dollar-value-add for the Trust. The March 26 board meeting placed potential IPS amendments on the agenda alongside asset allocation target review. Current targets (effective October 1, 2025) are: Public Equity 35%, Private Equity 20%, Credit 21%, Real Assets 18%, Tactical Opportunities 6%. NEPC was tasked with a trustee risk tolerance survey in 2026 to inform efficient frontier portfolio construction for the next allocation cycle.

Colorado PERA ($60B) approved an amended CAP Statement of Investment Policy at its January 23, 2026 Investment Committee meeting and brought a private equity incentive fee phase-in to the March 19 agenda. The proposed structure raises excess return targets over five years: 1-year hurdles increase from 50bps to 70bps, 3-year from 30bps to 40bps, and 5-year from 25bps to 30bps. The fund also established a standalone Private Credit asset class in January 2025 (carved out from the Opportunistic bucket) with a 6% Alternatives allocation target, while noting that one real estate property was reclassified from Core to Opportunistic after leverage exceeded the 50% limit, reaching 75–76%.

## **Performance Data: Strong 2025 Results, Mixed Early-2026 Signals**

Performance data embedded in 2026 board packs reveals a broadly strong 2025 for most funds, with mixed results in the first two months of 2026.

IPERS returned 13.75% for CY2025 against a 13.40% benchmark — a 35 basis point edge. TMRS delivered 13.29% in 2025, exceeding benchmark by 107bps and adding $5.6B in value. LACERA's Risk Reduction and Mitigation portfolio — a 24% fund allocation spanning Investment Grade Bonds, Long-Term Government Bonds, Hedge Funds, and Cash — returned 6.8% for the trailing one year as of February 28, 2026, versus a 6.0% benchmark, with all sub-components meeting or exceeding their respective benchmarks.

Oregon PERS ($110B) was a notable outlier: the total fund returned 10.67% for the trailing one year ending February 2026, underperforming its policy benchmark of 14.58% by 391 basis points — a gap large enough to drive an April 3 board discussion of earnings crediting and reserving for CY2025. Minnesota SBI ($161B) reported $161 billion in total AUM and $107.6 billion in Combined Funds as of December 31, 2025, though specific return data was not itemized in the March 26 board pack.

## **Governance, ESG, and Leadership Transitions**

Governance transitions, ESG policy, and leadership succession featured prominently across the first quarter of 2026.

IMRF ($50B) is navigating a leadership gap following the departure of Executive Director Brian Collins. The March 27 board meeting included appointment of an Interim Executive Director and formation of a formal Executive Director Search Committee. The Wisconsin Retirement System ($130B) similarly initiated a search for a new ETF Secretary, with recruitment strategy discussed in closed session at the April 3 Executive Committee meeting.

Colorado PERA added a Restricted Company List related to companies with prohibitions against Israel as a consent agenda item at its March 20, 2026 board meeting — a direct geopolitical divestment action reflecting continued pressure on public pension funds to make explicit ESG-linked exclusion decisions.

NYCERS ($88B) renewed its membership in the Ceres Investor Network on Climate Risk (INCR) at its April 9 meeting for $13,995 annually, reaffirming its climate engagement posture with a modest but visible governance signal.

LACERA formalized a new fiduciary counsel panel and ratified labor agreements with SEIU bargaining units at its April 1 Board of Retirement meeting. LACERS ($25B) approved a Succession Policy for the General Manager position in March 2026 in response to a 2022 Management Audit recommendation. OCERS ($25B) flagged Alternative Investment Vehicles as a priority audit emphasis area in its 2026 Risk Assessment and Audit Plan — specifically covering controls over capital calls, investment returns, and fair value assessments with sample confirmations from investment managers. The Houston Police Officers' Pension System undertook a concurrent review of its Risk Control Policy at the April 7 Investment Committee, while the Administrative Committee separately reviewed the Electronic Communications Policy.
