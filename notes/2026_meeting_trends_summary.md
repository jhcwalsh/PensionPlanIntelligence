## Private Markets Deployment: PE, Credit, Real Assets, and Secondaries

Private markets capital deployment dominated board agendas across the sample in early 2026, with commitment volumes reflecting continued conviction in illiquid strategies despite elevated entry multiples and macro uncertainty.

The most aggressive deployer in the sample was **LACERA ($75B, CA)**, which approved or disclosed over **$2.2 billion** in new commitments across a six-week span. CIO Jonathan Grabel authorized a **$750 million** multi-asset credit managed account with **Neuberger Berman**, a **$400 million** commitment to **Green Equity Investors X, L.P.** (Leonard Green's North America buyout fund), **$400 million** into **PGIM Real Estate's PRISA** open-end core fund, **$350 million** to **AE Industrial Partners IV, L.P.** (industrials/materials buyout), **$150 million** to **Regal Healthcare Capital Partners IV**, and **$100 million** to **Otro Capital Fund I** (sports, media, and entertainment). The fund also executed a **$412 million** rebalance into the **Clarion Lion Properties Fund** and approved two co-investments: **$41 million** alongside **Kingswood Capital Management** and **$25 million** alongside **Primary Venture Partners**. The pace and scale confirm LACERA's intent to rapidly close private markets allocation gaps created by the 2024 Strategic Asset Allocation, which expanded the Risk Reduction and Mitigation portfolio target from 19% to 24%.

**Minnesota State Board of Investment ($161B, MN)** ratified eleven private markets commitments totaling up to **$1.975 billion** at its October 2025 meeting—the largest single-meeting batch in the sample—spanning **Advent International GPE XI** (**$150M**), **Blackstone Supplemental Account – M** (**$300M**), **Strategic Partners X** (**$250M**), **Bridgepoint Europe VIII** (**$200M**), **ECP VI** (**$250M**), **Nordic Capital XII** (**$150M**), **Permira IX** (**$250M**), **TA Realty Value-Add Fund XIV** (**$150M**), **TPG Partners X** (**$150M**), **Wind Point Partners XI** (**$200M**), and **Merit Capital Fund VIII** (**$125M**). The breadth of geographies—pan-European, U.S. mid-market, growth equity, and real estate value-add—reflects MN SBI's policy-driven pacing under a newly adopted Investment Policy Statement incorporating its 2025 Asset Allocation Study.

**Washington State Investment Board ($200B, WA)** considered three commitments at its April 2 Private Markets Committee: **Charterhouse Capital Partners XII** (pan-European mid-market buyout, targeting **€1.5–2.0 billion**), **Spark Capital IX** and **Spark Capital Growth VI** (venture/venture growth, combined firm AUM **$8.7 billion**), and **Monarch Capital Partners VII** (private credit/distressed). Charterhouse's CCP X generated a **20% net IRR** and **2.0x TVPI**; CCP XI posted **15% net IRR** at preliminary figures as of December 31, 2025. WSIB's pairing of European mid-market buyout with early-stage U.S. venture in a single committee session illustrates the multi-vintage diversification approach institutionalized at the largest plans.

**Dallas Police & Fire Pension System ($3B, TX)** committed **$35 million** to **Blackstone Strategic Partners X** (PE secondaries, targeting **$22.5 billion**) and up to **$15 million** combined to **Lexington Continuation Vehicle Investors** and a related co-investment vehicle—both approved 9-1 with Trustee Duenas dissenting. **IPERS ($41B, IA)** formalized its CY2026 private markets commitment plan at **$2.025 billion** in aggregate maximums: **$750 million** to private real assets, **$700 million** to private equity, and **$575 million** to private credit. **HPOPS ($8B, TX)** evaluated commitments to **Ares Pathfinder III**, **Kingswood Capital Opportunities Fund IV**, and **LS Power Equity Partners VI** at its April 7 Investment Committee. **PSERS ($73B, PA)** approved commitments to **DRA Growth and Income Fund XII**, **LS Power Equity Partners VI**, and **Warwick Partners V** at its March 19 meeting, with a delegated authorization for **LLR Equity Partners** also approved.

In real estate, **OCERS ($25B, CA)** adopted a 2026 plan targeting **$50–$100 million** in core commitments and **$150–$225 million** in non-core, favoring data centers, built-to-rent, manufactured housing, and secondaries. The fund's investment consultant **Townsend Group** increased long-term core real estate return expectations by 50 basis points to **6.5%** and value-add by 200 basis points to **8.5%**, underpinning the non-core overweight. **SERS PA ($35B, PA)** evaluated **Francisco Partners Fund VIII** and **Francisco Partners Agility Fund IV** at its March 3 board meeting.

## Manager Hires and Mandate Changes

New manager selections concentrated in fixed income alternatives, public equity, real estate, and infrastructure, with several plans executing simultaneous searches across multiple asset classes.

**IPERS** made the most significant multi-manager hiring decision in the sample, approving three absolute return/portable alpha managers at its December 2025 meeting: **Florin Court Capital** (Florin Court Capital Programme), **Capital Fund Management SA** (CFM ISTEC Program), and **AHL Partners LLP** (AHL Cat Bond Program)—all subject to due diligence and contract negotiations. The hires were driven by CEO Greg Samorajski's concern that IPERS' active risk tracking error had fallen to just **23 basis points** against a target of **150 bps** and upper limit of **300 bps**, leaving substantial active risk budget unused. IPERS also hired **BentallGreenOak (BGO)** for U.S. Core Plus real estate and **CBRE Group, Inc.** for U.S. Core real estate, both following a competitive RFP, replacing exposure the fund needed to rebuild after eliminating its 5% Global Smart Beta allocation under Portfolio C.

**LACERS ($25B, CA)** approved 1-year contracts with **Granahan Investment Management** (U.S. Small Cap Growth, **~$160.8 million** portfolio) and **Copeland Capital Management** (U.S. Small Cap Core, **~$297.3 million** portfolio), both renewals recommended by **NEPC** despite persistent underperformance—Granahan returned **2.38%** versus a **13.01%** benchmark for FY2025; Copeland returned **-1.04%** versus **12.81%**. Copeland has been on watch since March 2025. Both renewals were limited to one year, preserving termination optionality. LACERS also renewed its contract with **Dimensional Fund Advisors LP** for Active Non-U.S. Emerging Markets Value Equities (3-year term) and approved an **~$800 million** RFP for Global Listed Infrastructure management covering both active and passive mandates, with proposals due June 4, 2026.

**SCERS ($12B, CA)** approved a **$450 million** allocation to **Man Numeric** for a Global Core ex-US Strategy, subject to contract negotiations. **LACERS ($75B, CA)** launched a formal RFP for a Real Estate investment consultant to replace or re-compete **StepStone Group LP**, which received only a one-year extension of its private equity consulting agreement at the April 8 board meeting. Separately, the passive cash overlay investment manager search advanced to contract development, with finalists identified as **Legal & General Asset Management**, **NISA Investment Advisors**, **Parametric Portfolio Associates**, **Russell Investments**, and **State Street Investment Management**.

## Portfolio Strategy Shifts: Allocation Reviews, Policy Changes, and Benchmark Updates

Several funds executed or signaled meaningful structural shifts to asset allocation, benchmarks, and risk frameworks in the first half of 2026.

**IPERS** adopted Portfolio C at its December 2025 meeting (7-0), eliminating the 5% Global Smart Beta allocation and redirecting **2%** to core fixed income and **3%** to public credit. The reallocation addresses persistent benchmark-hugging and low active risk while improving fixed income diversification. The active risk discussion continued at the March 27 board meeting with a dedicated presentation from consultant **Wilshire Advisors** (Thomas Toth and Ali Kazemi).

**Colorado PERA ($60B, CO)** advanced a Private Equity Incentive Phase-in proposal at its March 19 Investment Committee that would increase excess return targets over five years: from 50 to 70 basis points (1-year), 30 to 40 basis points (3-year), and 25 to 30 basis points (5-year). The fund established a Private Credit asset class in January 2025 as a carve-out from its Opportunistic bucket and set an Alternatives allocation target of 6% of total fund. Real estate compliance exceptions were significant: five directly-owned properties exceeded the **50% leverage limit** in 2025, with one property reaching **76.16%** leverage due to adverse market valuations; one property was reclassified from Core to Opportunistic as a result.

**TMRS ($40B, TX)** reported a **13.29%** total fund return for FY2025, exceeding its benchmark by **107 basis points**, and is undergoing an Investment Policy Statement amendment review with asset allocation targets of: Public Equity 35%, Private Equity 20%, Credit 21%, Real Assets 18%, and Tactical Opportunities 6%. NEPC is conducting a trustee risk tolerance survey in 2026 to inform efficient frontier modeling. TMRS's actuarial assumed return of **6.75%** is below Texas peers ERS and TRS at 7.0% and TCDRS at 7.25%.

**CalPERS ($502B, CA)** had a Total Fund Policy Review listed as a first reading at its March 16–18 board meetings, alongside an Asset-Liability Management review covering both Affiliate Funds and the Public Employees' Retirement Fund implementation strategy, advised by **Wilshire Advisors**. A Reference Portfolio Benchmark Recommendation was also on the closed-session Investment Committee agenda—signaling a potential structural recalibration of the fund's passive reference point.

**LACERA ($75B)** increased its RRM target allocation from 19% to 24% in the 2024 Strategic Asset Allocation. The **$22.26 billion** RRM portfolio (24% of total fund) provides **37 months** of benefit payments in liquidity (excluding Hedge Funds), comprising Investment Grade Bonds (**$11.7B**, 70% passive/30% active, effective duration 5.8 years), Long-Term Government Bonds (**$1.7B**, effective duration 13.9 years), Hedge Funds (**$7.5B**), and Cash (**$1.3B**). The hedge fund benchmark was revised in July 2024 from T-Bills +250 bps to T-Bills +200 bps. **OCERS** adopted MSCI private markets benchmarks in 2025 and expanded its co-investment program to asset classes beyond real estate.

## Performance Data: Fund Returns vs. Benchmarks

CY2025 and fiscal year-end performance data was broadly strong across the sample, with most funds outperforming policy benchmarks driven by public equity gains and natural resources.

**IPERS** led the sample in reported CY2025 total fund performance at **13.75%** versus a **13.40%** benchmark, with fund value reaching **$49.4 billion**. International equity was a standout at **33.21%** versus a **32.39%** benchmark; U.S. equity returned **17.78%** versus **17.15%**. The Liquid Alternatives Risk Strategy (LARS) was the notable laggard at **-6.88%** versus a **4.18%** benchmark, with manager-level concerns flagged for **Welton Global**. Private real assets returned **4.13%** in line with benchmark; private credit returned **7.46%** matching benchmark. Fixed-income manager concerns flagged for **Man** and **Mesirow**.

**TMRS** reported **13.29%** total fund return for FY2025, exceeding its benchmark by **107 basis points** and adding **$5.6 billion** to the Trust. TMRS achieved GIPS verification for the fourth consecutive year—the first Texas public pension to do so—and its 5-year rolling return reached the **38th percentile** among peers.

**LACERA's** Real Assets ex-Real Estate portfolio returned **12.1%** for the one-year period ending December 31, 2025, against a **7.6%** custom benchmark—a **450 basis point** outperformance. Natural Resources was the strongest sub-component at **17.8%** versus a **4.1%** benchmark, driven by record gold demand and AI/electrification metals demand. Infrastructure co-investments returned **23.1%** since inception (March 2023) versus a **9.9%** benchmark. The RRM portfolio returned **6.8%** versus a **6.0%** benchmark for the 1-year period ending February 2026.

**LACERA's** total pension fund returned **9.7%** for FY2025 (year ended June 30, 2025); the OPEB Trust returned **11.1%** for the same period. PERA's pension UAAL stands at **14.63%** with a **7.00%** assumed rate and **$607 million** Star Reserve.

**SCERS ($12B, CA)** reported an **11.1%** total fund return in FY2024-2025 against a **6.75%** discount rate assumption, reducing the Net Pension Liability from **$1,692.4 million** to **$1,019.7 million**—a **$672.7 million** improvement. **OCERS** reported a total fund return of **11.3%** against a **7.0%** benchmark year-to-date through mid-November 2025. **IPERS** funded status improved from **90.75%** to **92.17%** per its 2025 actuarial valuation, with the unfunded actuarial liability declining from **$4.38 billion** to **$3.84 billion**.

**LACERS' Granahan** and **Copeland** mandates represented the clearest underperformance cases in the sample: Granahan posted **2.38%** versus a **13.01%** Russell 2000 Growth benchmark for FY2025; Copeland returned **-1.04%** versus **12.81%** for the same period, with both managers on NEPC's watch or focus list. Colorado PERA's **PERAdvantage International Stock Fund** underperformed materially in Q4 2025 at **3.6%** versus a **5.1%** benchmark.

## Governance, ESG, and Leadership Transitions

Governance activity was particularly dense in the sample, with leadership transitions, policy modernization, cybersecurity initiatives, and ESG-adjacent legislative positions all appearing across multiple plans.

**Leadership transitions** were a recurring theme. At **LACERA**, multiple senior officers retired in March 2026: Assistant Executive Officer Laura Guglielmo, Chief Counsel Vincent Lim (whose Disability Litigation Office was absorbed into the Legal Office), Division Manager Bernie Buenaflor, and Assistant Division Manager Leilani Ignacio. **Leisha Collins** was appointed Chief Internal Audit in a dual vote: **8-0** by the Board of Retirement (March 4) and **8-0** by the Board of Investments (March 11). CIO Jonathan Grabel introduced newly hired Senior Investment Analysts Kunal Gawande, Tolkyn Takishova, and Shawn Kim. At **IPERS**, Deputy CEO Rick Hindman announced his retirement effective January 2026, with direct reports consolidated under CEO Greg Samorajski. At **TMRS**, Director of Actuarial Services Leslee Hardy retired April 30 after 18 years. **LACERS** adopted a formal Succession Policy to govern General Manager vacancies, specifying an order of succession: Executive Officer, Assistant General Manager, Chief Investment Officer.

**ESG and divestment actions** generated notable governance debate. **Colorado PERA** included divestment from companies with prohibitions against Israel on its March 20 consent agenda, and is tracking an Anti-BDS 50 bps calculation as a routine governance item. At a prior January meeting, the board voted **12-2** to adopt CEO performance criteria without incorporating "relative to liabilities" investment measurement language—defeating the amendment **1-13**. **LACERA** submitted a comment letter to the UK Financial Conduct Authority regarding proposed sustainability reporting requirements and adopted an AI Policy Public Summary (approved **7-0** at the March 4 Board of Retirement), requiring human oversight for all final decisions and prohibiting sensitive data in unapproved AI tools. **WSIB's** Charterhouse commitment includes a manager certified as a **B Corp** (May 2025) with net-zero by 2030 targets.

**Fiduciary counsel and legal governance** saw significant activity. LACERA's RFP for fiduciary counsel drew seven proposals; four semifinalists were interviewed; the board selected a three-firm panel—**Klausner Kaufman Jensen & Levinson** ($500/hr), **Nossaman LLP** ($600–$720/hr), and **Reed Smith LLP**—for a five-year term, approved unanimously by both the Board of Retirement and Board of Investments. **PSERS** received an update on the **Verus/Cerity merger** affecting its investment consultant relationship and approved a new Public Information Policy. **OCERS** directed immediate recruitment for its vacant Director of Internal Audit position. **DPFP** is advancing a **$425,000** pre-engagement phase with LRS to replace its 23-year-old PensionGold v2 pension administration software.

**Technology and AI initiatives** emerged as a cross-plan theme. LACERA has Microsoft Copilot deployed organization-wide and active AI projects including a lacera.gov chatbot, AI-enhanced disability retirement medical report organization, and AI-assisted legal/contract research. The fund's **$920,000** mainframe migration targets June 2028 completion; a **$960,000** Business Intelligence platform (Microsoft Fabric/Power BI) targets December 2027. **OCERS** is piloting AI tools for fee verification and cash flow modeling. **Colorado PERA** identified AI in investments as a top-priority trustee education topic for 2026, alongside cybersecurity risk oversight. **CalPERS** scheduled a geopolitical education session with Lieutenant General David Bellon alongside its Reference Portfolio Benchmark review, and has active litigation: **BDO USA, PC v. CalPERS** (Sacramento County Superior Court, Case No. 26WM000055) in closed session.

**Legislative activity** was concentrated in California. AB 1619 would raise CERL board member compensation from $100 to up to $320 per meeting—unchanged in over 40 years—with LACERA supporting the bill if amended to include Board of Investments trustees. AB 2780 (omnibus CERL technical updates, co-sponsored by CalPERS and CalSTRS) received LACERA's support. California's SB 827, effective January 1, 2026, added a two-hour fiscal and financial training requirement for county retirement board members; **SCERS** amended its Board Education Policy accordingly. TMRS is planning for the 2027 Texas Legislative Session and identified hiring a Director of Governmental Relations as a critical staffing need, with four trustees requiring Senate Nominations Committee confirmation.
