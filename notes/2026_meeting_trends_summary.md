## Private Markets Deployment: Commitments, Co-Investments, and Pacing

Private markets deployment dominated board calendars across virtually every plan in the sample, with aggregate new commitments running well into the tens of billions of dollars. The period reflects a deliberate acceleration of pacing by plans that remain structurally underweight relative to target allocations.

**Los Angeles County Employees Retirement Association ($75B, CA)** was among the most active deployers, with the CIO executing commitments totaling approximately **$1.8 billion** in January 2026 alone, followed by an additional **$416 million** in three private equity transactions approved in March. The January tranche included a **$750 million** multi-asset credit mandate to **Neuberger Berman** via managed account, a **$400 million** commitment to **Green Equity Investors X, L.P.** (Leonard Green & Partners' global buyout vehicle focused on consumer, healthcare, and industrials), **$400 million** into **PRISA** (PGIM Real Estate open-end core fund), **$150 million** to **Regal Healthcare Capital Partners IV, L.P.**, and **$100 million** to **Otro Capital Fund I, L.P.** (sports, media, and entertainment). March additions included **$350 million** to **AE Industrial Partners IV, L.P.** (North American industrials buyout), a **$41 million** co-investment alongside **Kingswood Capital Management**, and a **$25 million** co-investment with **Primary Venture Partners**. The April board agenda added a further **$350 million** CIO-approved commitment to **AE Industrial Partners IV** alongside smaller co-investments — reflecting LACERA's high-velocity deployment model via delegated CIO authority within pre-approved parameters.

**Minnesota State Board of Investment ($161B, MN)** approved eleven private markets commitments totaling up to **$1.975 billion** at its October 2025 meeting, ratified in the March 2026 review period. Notable allocations include **$300 million** to **Blackstone Supplemental Account – M, L.P.**, **$250 million** each to **Strategic Partners X** (Blackstone secondaries) and **ECP VI, L.P.** (energy/infrastructure), **$250 million** to **Permira IX**, **$200 million** each to **Bridgepoint Europe VIII** and **Wind Point Partners XI**, and smaller commitments to **Advent International GPE XI** (**$150 million**), **Nordic Capital XII** (**$150 million**), **TPG Partners X** (**$150 million**), **TA Realty Value-Add Fund XIV** (**$150 million**), and **Merit Capital Fund VIII** (**$125 million**). The breadth and scale of this single-meeting batch underscores the SBI's programmatic commitment pacing.

**Washington State Investment Board ($200B, WA)** advanced commitments to **Charterhouse Capital Partners XII, L.P.** — a pan-European mid-market buyout fund targeting **€1.5–2.0 billion** with a focus on Services and Healthcare — alongside **Spark Capital IX, L.P.** and **Spark Capital Growth VI, L.P.** (venture and venture growth, combined firm AUM **$8.7 billion**), and **Monarch Capital Partners VII, L.P.** under its Innovation/private credit sleeve. Charterhouse's predecessor fund CCP X generated a **20% net IRR** and **2.0x TVPI** on realized investments, providing strong underwriting support.

**Dallas Police & Fire Pension System ($3B, TX)** approved two private equity secondaries commitments — **$35 million** to **Blackstone Strategic Partners X** and up to **$15 million** to **Lexington Continuation Vehicle Investors** — on a 9-1 vote (Trustee Duenas opposed). These moves reflect DPFP's effort to close a significant allocation gap: private markets represent **11.2% of assets ($245 million)** against an **18% target ($393 million)**, a **-$148 million/-6.9% variance**. Staff and advisor **Albourne** anticipate **four to six additional private markets commitments** throughout 2026 per the pacing plan.

**Houston Police Officers' Pension System ($8B, TX)** had its Investment Committee reviewing three simultaneous commitments — to **Ares Pathfinder III, L.P.**, **Kingswood Capital Opportunities Fund IV, L.P.**, and **LS Power Equity Partners VI, L.P.** — alongside a real estate transaction at **1600 Smith**. The Kingswood overlap with LACERA's co-investment program illustrates how mid-market managers are accessing multiple institutional LPs concurrently.

**Pennsylvania School Employees' Retirement System ($73B, PA)** approved commitments to **DRA Growth and Income Fund XII, LLC**, **LS Power Equity Partners VI, L.P.** (also appearing at HPOPS), and **Warwick Partners V, L.P.**, along with a delegated authorization for **LLR Equity Partners** — demonstrating continued appetite for diversified private equity even amid a Verus/Cerity consultant merger creating some organizational uncertainty.

**Iowa Public Employees' Retirement System ($41B, IA)** adopted a **CY2026 private markets commitment plan** calling for maximum commitments of **$750 million** to private real assets, **$700 million** to private equity, and **$575 million** to private credit — a total of **$2.025 billion** — while noting that private credit and private real assets remain below target allocations. Real estate hirings include **BentallGreenOak** (U.S. Core Plus) and **CBRE Group, Inc.** (U.S. Core), both approved at the December 2025 board meeting.

**North Dakota State Investment Board ($8B, ND)** reported its **$85 million** commitment to **PRISA III** (U.S. value-add real estate, approved October 2024) as fully invested. The board also funded **Arrowstreet US All Cap** with approximately **$790 million** across the Legacy Fund (**$450 million**), Pension Pool (**$300 million**), and Insurance Trust (**$40 million**) — an unusually large public markets transaction reflecting portfolio restructuring under the Equity 2.0 framework.

Across plans, **co-investments and secondaries** are increasingly prominent as fee-mitigation tools. **CalPERS ($502B, CA)** reported that **41% of its $13.8 billion** in first-half FY2026 PE commitments were deployed through no- or low-fee vehicles including co-investments and Customized Investment Accounts (CIAs). The CIA structure returned **25.1%** on a one-year basis versus **12.9%** for buyouts and **-0.5%** for Fund of Funds — a performance differential that is systematically reshaping commitment structures at the largest plans.

## Manager Hires and Mandate Changes

Manager activity was substantial and skewed toward equity, credit, and real assets mandates, with several terminations and restructurings alongside new hires.

**WSIB** hired **Life Cycle Investment Partners** for a Global Select Equity mandate, the sole public equity manager hire in the reviewed period among major plans. **LACERS ($25B, CA)** renewed **Dimensional Fund Advisors LP** for a three-year term on its **$744 million** Active Non-U.S. Emerging Markets Value Equities portfolio (benchmark: MSCI Emerging Markets Value Index, effective July 1, 2026 through June 30, 2029, approved 5-0), and separately approved one-year contracts with **Granahan Investment Management** (Active U.S. Small Cap Growth) and **Copeland Capital Management** (Active U.S. Small Cap Core). LACERS also launched an RFP for Global Listed Infrastructure portfolio management.

**Sacramento County Employees' Retirement System ($12B, CA)** approved a **$450 million** investment with **Man Numeric** for a Global Core ex-US Strategy, authorized by board vote with contract negotiations pending. **IPERS** hired three absolute return/portable alpha managers from an RFP process: **Florin Court Capital** (Florin Court Capital Programme), **Capital Fund Management SA** (CFM ISTEC Program), and **AHL Partners LLP** (AHL Cat Bond Program) — all subject to due diligence and contract negotiation, approved 7-0.

**SIB-ND** terminated **William Blair International Leaders** in early March 2026 and funded **Principal International** with approximately **$407 million** (**$243 million** Non-Qualified Pool and **$164 million** Pension Pool) as a replacement. The board also approved a new internally managed Long Government/Credit Enhanced Index Strategy of approximately **$42 million**, tracking the Bloomberg US Long Government/Credit Bond Index.

On the consulting side, **LACERA** approved a one-year extension to its private equity consulting agreement with **StepStone Group LP** and selected a new three-firm fiduciary counsel panel — **Klausner Kaufman Jensen & Levinson** (**$500/hour**), **Nossaman LLP** (**$600–$720/hour**), and **Reed Smith LLP** — for a five-year term. **Kentucky Public Pensions Authority ($20B, KY)** recommended hiring **Albourne America LLC** to perform investment fee validation services at **$2,000 per fund** for since-inception validation, following a competitive RFP with finalist presentations from **bFinance** (contingent 35% of savings fee model) and **XTP Implementation Services** (success-based). The preference for Albourne was supported by its existing KPPA/CERS relationship and its dedicated Fee and Liquidity team's analysis of over **7,000 investments** and **$200 billion** in validated client assets.

**Cook County Pension Fund ($12B, IL)** was in finalist evaluation for an International Small Cap Equity manager search. **DPFP** had Global Equity Core/Value RFP responses under evaluation by staff and advisor **Meketa Investment Group**, with a U.S. Small Cap manager search and a second Multi-Asset Credit search planned for later in 2026.

## Portfolio Strategy Shifts: Allocation Reviews, TPA, and Structural Evolution

The most analytically significant trend across the sample is a systemic shift in how large plans conceptualize portfolio construction — moving from static asset class buckets toward integrated risk frameworks, with implications for benchmarking, fee structures, and manager relationships.

**CalPERS** is formally transitioning to a **Total Portfolio Approach (TPA)**, which the board described as fundamentally shifting the capital allocation framework across all asset classes. Private equity — with a current NAV of **$110.8 billion** (18.5% of Total Fund, above the 17% long-term target) — is expected to retain an important role within TPA, but the allocation logic will be governed by total portfolio risk contribution rather than asset class targets.

**SIB-ND** outlined a three-phase roadmap toward **Advanced Funds Management (AFM)**: Phase 1 (Foundation, current), Phase 2 (Integrated Risk Control), and Phase 3 (Total Portfolio Optimization and Capital Efficiency), with overlay and portable alpha as Phase 3 goals. The board is explicitly separating alpha from market exposures, exploring AI tools for portfolio monitoring and ultimately investment recommendations, and transitioning private markets from fund-of-funds to direct-to-GP relationships for fee savings. A **Fixed Income 2.0** initiative is underway following the completion of the successful **Equity 2.0** program, which returned **18.0%** in 2025 versus a **17.1%** benchmark.

**IPERS** adopted **Portfolio C**, eliminating the 5% Global Smart Beta allocation and reallocating **2%** to core fixed income and **3%** to public credit — a structural simplification. The board also flagged a significant active risk concern: tracking error of just **23 basis points** against a **150 basis point target** and a **300 basis point upper limit**, prompting a multi-meeting policy review.

**Colorado PERA ($60B, CO)** is moving forward with a **Private Equity Incentive Phase-in**, proposing to raise excess return hurdle targets from **50 bps to 70 bps** (one-year), **30 bps to 40 bps** (three-year), and **25 bps to 30 bps** (five-year) phased over five years — a governance mechanism to align internal PE compensation with stronger outperformance expectations. The fund also established a new **Private Credit asset class** (January 2025) with an **Alternatives target of 6%** of the total fund, carved out from the Opportunistic bucket.

**TMRS ($40B, TX)** reported its current target allocation as Public Equity **35%**, Private Equity **20%**, Credit **21%**, Real Assets **18%**, and Tactical Opportunities **6%**, with an IPS amendment under consideration. The board is conducting a trustee risk appetite survey via **NEPC** to inform efficient frontier modeling, and is reviewing assumptions underlying its **6.75% assumed rate of return** — below the **7.0%** rates used by Texas ERS and TRS, and the **7.25%** assumed by TCDRS.

**LACERA** approved an RFP for Global Listed Infrastructure and is transitioning Real Assets public market completion portfolios to sunset by 2027, with commodity exposures fully liquidated by end of 2024. Its **Risk Reduction and Mitigation (RRM)** portfolio target was increased from 19% to 24% in the 2024 Strategic Asset Allocation, now comprising Investment Grade Bonds (13%), Long-Term Government Bonds (2%), Hedge Funds (8%), and Cash (1%) — a market value of **$22.26 billion** providing 37 months of benefit payment liquidity.

**DPFP** is conducting a full asset allocation study with **Meketa**, with a final proposal to the board expected Q3/Q4 2026. Fixed Income and Cash is significantly overweight at **19.4%** versus a **13% target**, and Private Equity is severely underweight at **1.8%** versus a **6% target**, driving the aggressive secondaries pacing described above.

## Performance Data: Returns Across Funds

CY2025 and trailing multi-year performance was broadly strong across public and private markets, though early 2026 data shows a sharp equity market reversal with broad implications for near-term returns.

**IPERS** was the standout total fund performer in the reviewed period, returning **13.75%** for CY2025 against a policy benchmark of **13.40%**, with outperformance across virtually every asset class. International equity led at **33.21%** versus a **32.39%** benchmark; U.S. equity returned **17.78%** versus **17.15%**; public credit returned **10.82%** versus **10.22%**. The sole significant laggard was the LARS (Liquid Alternatives Risk Strategy) portfolio, which returned **-6.88%** against a **4.18%** benchmark — a meaningful drag motivating the absolute return manager hirings described above. Three-year total fund return was **10.86%** versus a **10.64%** benchmark; five-year return was **7.70%** versus **7.46%**.

**TMRS** reported a **13.29%** total fund return for FY2025, outperforming its benchmark by **107 basis points** and adding **$5.6 billion** to the Trust — the fund's fourth consecutive year of achieving GIPS verification. TMRS's five-year rolling returns have improved to the **38th percentile** among peers since implementing a higher-return asset allocation in mid-2021.

**CalPERS** private equity returned **17.8%** for the one-year period ending December 31, 2025, slightly trailing the policy benchmark of **18.8%**, with the Growth/Expansion strategy at **30.2%** and Venture at **20.4%** outpacing Buyouts (**12.9%**) and Credit (**8.6%**). The Fund of Funds structure returned **-0.5%** and Emerging Markets **-5.6%**, both persistent underperformers. The three-year PE return of **12.8%** significantly trailed the benchmark of **24.9%** — a differential the report partially attributes to the lagged valuation effects of 2022–2023 vintages.

**LACERA**'s RRM portfolio delivered **6.8%** for the one-year period ending February 2026 versus a **6.0%** benchmark. The Real Assets ex-Real Estate portfolio returned **12.1%** for the one-year period versus a custom benchmark of **7.6%**, with Natural Resources especially strong at **17.8%** versus a **4.1%** benchmark driven by gold and industrial metals demand. Natural Resources co-investments returned **30.2%** against a **6.4%** benchmark since inception. Infrastructure co-investments generated **23.1%** since inception versus a **9.9%** benchmark. Long-Term Government Bonds showed an inception-to-date return of **-6.4%** annualized (inception November 2021) due to the 2022 rate spike.

**SCERS ($12B, CA)** reported total fund returns of **11.1%** for FY2024–2025 versus the **6.75%** actuarial assumption, driving a **$672.7 million** decrease in Net Pension Liability to **$1,019.7 million** and a pension expense drop from **$251.8 million** to **$36.3 million**.

**MN SBI**'s Combined Funds ranked **top quartile** or better versus public pension peers across all measured time periods (1, 3, 5, 10, and 20 years) in TUCS, with a **10-year return of 9.9%** versus a **9.7%** benchmark as of December 31, 2025.

The macroeconomic backdrop deteriorated sharply in Q1 2026: the **S&P 500 was down 4.3% YTD** and the **Russell 3000 down 4.0% as of March 31, 2026**, while international developed markets (MSCI World ex-US) declined only **0.9%**. **DPFP** reported a total fund return of **-2.41%** through March 31, 2026, with the public portfolio down **1.7%**. The Fed held rates steady at **3.50%–3.75%** at its March 2026 meeting (second consecutive pause), with CPI at **2.4%** year-over-year and PPI at **3.4%**. Core PCE remained sticky at approximately **3.1%**, and Q4 GDP growth slowed to approximately **0.7%**, creating a stagflationary undertone in the macro discussion across multiple board rooms.

## Governance, ESG, and Leadership Transitions

Governance activity was unusually concentrated in the review period, with leadership changes, policy modernization, and ESG-adjacent legislative responses all appearing prominently.

**Leadership transitions** were notable at multiple plans. **LACERA** appointed **Leisha Collins** as Chief Internal Audit (8-0 vote, approved simultaneously by Board of Investments and Board of Retirement). **IPERS** Deputy CEO **Rick Hindman** announced retirement effective January 2026, with direct reports now reporting to CEO **Greg Samorajski**. **TMRS** Director of Actuarial Services **Leslee Hardy** is retiring April 30, 2026 after 18 years, attending her final board meeting in March. **LACERS** introduced new Assistant General Manager **Rahoof Oyewole** and adopted a formal Succession Policy establishing order of succession as: Executive Officer → Assistant General Manager → Chief Investment Officer — incorporated into Board Governance Manual Article II, Section 3.6. **LACERA** (county plan) hired Senior Investment Analysts **Kunal Gawande**, **Tolkyn Takishova**, and **Shawn Kim** during the review period.

On **ESG and investment policy**, **Colorado PERA** updated its Restricted Company List related to companies with prohibitions against Israel as a consent agenda item, continuing a pattern observed across Texas-area and Colorado plans. PERA also defeated a board motion (1-13) to amend CEO evaluation criteria to measure investment performance "relative to liabilities" and "based on actuarial value returns" — a significant governance signal about the board majority's preference for market-value performance measurement. **WSIB** held an energy transition investing discussion and reviewed its Economically Targeted Investments Annual Report, while Charterhouse Capital Partners — a WSIB PE commitment — earned B Corp certification in May 2025 with a net-zero-by-2030 target. **LACERA** submitted a comment letter to the UK Financial Conduct Authority regarding proposed sustainability disclosure requirements, an unusual cross-border engagement reflecting the fund's global exposure.

**California legislative activity** generated multiple agenda items. **AB 1619** would raise board member compensation from **$100 to up to $320 per meeting** — a rate unchanged for over 40 years — with LACERA and SCERS both monitoring it (SCERS noted AB 1619 as a legislative update item). **AB 2780** (omnibus CERL clarification bill co-sponsored by SACRS, CalPERS, and CalSTRS) addresses trustee election eligibility, errors and omissions provisions, and compensation terminology. **LACERA** is pursuing four direct Charter reform proposals to the Los Angeles City Council covering staffing authority, meeting frequency, GM selection, and independent legal counsel.

**Technology and AI governance** emerged as a recurring theme. **LACERA** approved an AI Policy (8-0 at March 4 meeting), has deployed **Microsoft Copilot** organization-wide, and is developing AI applications including a member-facing chatbot, AI-enabled disability medical report organization, and AI-assisted investment analysis tools. **SIB-ND** is exploring AI tools specifically for investment analysis and portfolio monitoring, with an AI integration roadmap spanning from current productivity tools to eventual AI-driven investment recommendations. **Colorado PERA** identified AI in investments and technology governance as priority trustee education topics for 2026. The **LACERA** boardroom itself is being physically relocated from the 8th floor to the ground floor of Gateway Plaza at a **$700,000** budget with a target completion of March 2027.

**Audit and operational controls** saw meaningful attention. **LACERS** (city plan) received findings from its Internal Audit noting "passive acceptance" of custodian bank data without independent validation, recommending monthly variance analysis — a finding with implications for fee oversight and reconciliation accuracy. **Colorado PERA**'s audit identified five real estate properties with leverage compliance exceptions exceeding the 50% limit (one reaching 76.16%), two proxy voting errors, and approximately 750 proxy votes missed over three years during an account transition at Northern Trust. **PSERS** is navigating a consultant transition following the **Verus/Cerity merger**, while simultaneously running an actuarial services vendor selection (RFP 2025-04) and a healthcare third-party administrator RFP.
