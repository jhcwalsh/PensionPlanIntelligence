# 2026 Meeting Agenda Trends: Key Topics & Announcements

*Covering January–April 2026 across 19 major U.S. pension plans (~$2 trillion AUM)*

---

## Private Markets Deployment at Scale

Private equity dominates investment activity in 2026. LACERA committed over $1.8 billion across real estate, credit, and private equity in just two months, including a $750 million multi-asset credit mandate to Neuberger Berman, $400 million to PGIM Real Estate's PRISA core fund, and three PE commitments totalling $416 million (AE Industrial Partners IV, Kingswood, Primary Venture Partners). NJ, PSERS, and SERS all approved multiple PE commitments including Gridiron Capital, Wind Point Partners, Francisco Partners, Bain Capital Special Situations Asia, LS Power, and DRA Growth. Private credit is emerging as a distinct allocation, with Colorado PERA formally carving it out from its Opportunistic bucket.

## Strong 2025 Returns Dominate Q1 Reviews

Every plan with public performance data reported solid full-year 2025 results. CalPERS returned 15.4%, outperforming its benchmark by 70 basis points, driven by public equity (+22.5%), private equity (+17.8%), and private debt (+12.5%). OPERS posted +14.71% (DB) and +16.39% (HC). IPERS returned 13.75% versus a 13.40% benchmark. Several plans, including NYCERS and NYC Police, modestly underperformed policy benchmarks despite double-digit absolute returns, prompting active risk reviews.

## Portfolio Strategy Shifts

OPERS made the most significant allocation change, tripling its Risk Mitigation Strategies target from 3.5% to 10% by adding long treasuries, trend following, and alternative risk premia — and reducing core fixed income. CalPERS is transitioning to a Total Portfolio Approach (TPA) starting FY2026–27, replacing its SAA benchmark with a Reference Portfolio and redesigning staff incentive compensation to align with TPA performance.

## Macro Themes and Governance

Consultants across multiple plans flagged AI investment risks, tariff-driven geopolitical uncertainty, inflation, and K-shaped economy dynamics. CalPERS held a board education day on AI and cybersecurity. NYCERS restructured governance to hold independent investment meetings rather than joint sessions with BERS. LACERA adopted a formal AI Policy and launched searches for a new real estate consultant and cash overlay manager. Several plans are updating proxy voting frameworks and stewardship priorities.
