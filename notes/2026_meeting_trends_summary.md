## Private Markets Deployment: PE, Credit, Real Assets, and Secondaries

Private markets commitment activity in the first half of 2026 has been exceptionally dense, with dollar volumes and fund diversity suggesting an accelerating deployment cycle across fund sizes and geographies.

**LACERA ($75B, CA)** led the sample period in raw commitment volume. The CIO approved more than **$2.2 billion in new commitments across January–March 2026** alone, including **$750 million to Neuberger Berman** via a multi-asset credit managed account, **$400 million to Green Equity Investors X** (buyout, consumer/healthcare/industrials), **$400 million into PGIM Real Estate's PRISA** open-end core fund, **$350 million to AE Industrial Partners IV** (industrials/materials buyout), **$150 million to Regal Healthcare Capital Partners IV**, and **$100 million to Otro Capital Fund I** (sports, media, entertainment). Co-investments alongside **Kingswood Capital Management** ($41M) and **Primary Venture Partners** ($25M) added a further **$66 million**. The board simultaneously approved a Real Estate consultant RFP, with **StepStone Group LP** serving as incumbent while the process runs.

**Minnesota State Board of Investment ($161B, MN)** approved eleven private markets commitments totaling up to **$1.975 billion** at its October 2025 meeting — the largest single-meeting commitment slate in the dataset — encompassing buyout, growth equity, infrastructure energy, and real estate. Notable allocations included **$300 million to Blackstone Supplemental Account – M**, **$250 million each to Strategic Partners X and ECP VI**, **$250 million to Permira IX**, **$200 million each to Bridgepoint Europe VIII and Wind Point Partners XI**, and **$150 million each to Advent International GPE XI, Nordic Capital XII, and TA Realty Value-Add Fund XIV**.

**Washington State Investment Board ($200B, WA)** considered three private markets commitments in April 2026: **Charterhouse Capital Partners XII** (pan-European mid-market buyout, targeting €1.5–2.0bn with predecessor CCP X posting 20% net IRR / 2.0x TVPI), dual commitments to **Spark Capital IX** ($770M venture) and **Spark Capital Growth VI** ($1.5B venture growth), and **Monarch Capital Partners VII** under WSIB's Innovation Portfolio sleeve focused on private credit and distressed instruments. Charterhouse's B Corp certification and net-zero-by-2030 commitment made this one of the more ESG-integrated buyout presentations seen across the dataset.

**Houston Police Officers' Pension System ($8B, TX)** evaluated three private equity commitments simultaneously: **Ares Pathfinder III**, **Kingswood Capital Opportunities Fund IV**, and **LS Power Equity Partners VI** — the last of which also appeared at **PSERS ($73B, PA)**, where it was approved alongside **DRA Growth and Income Fund XII** and **Warwick Partners V**. The cross-plan appearance of LS Power VI reflects GP fundraising strategy targeting multiple public LP relationships within a compressed timeline.

**Dallas Police & Fire Pension System ($3B, TX)** approved **$35 million to Blackstone Strategic Partners X** (PE secondaries, targeting $22.5B) and **$15 million to Lexington Continuation Vehicle Investors** by a 9-1 vote, the lone dissent from trustee Duenas. These secondaries commitments follow an Albourne-led PE secondaries search, though Albourne disclosed a conflict of interest — advising an affiliate of Blackstone — triggering a gifts-and-entertainment disclosure covering 2022–2025.

**Iowa Public Employees' Retirement System ($41B, IA)** approved a **2026 private markets commitment plan** with maximum pacing targets of **$750 million to private real assets, $700 million to private equity, and $575 million to private credit** ($2.025 billion combined), adopted at the December 2025 meeting. Private credit and real assets remained below policy targets as of year-end, suggesting deployment headroom.

**Orange County Employees Retirement System ($25B, CA)** adopted a **2026 Real Estate Investment Plan** targeting **$200–$325 million** in new commitments (core and non-core combined), supplemented by a separately approved **$350 million real assets pacing plan** and **$400 million per year private income strategy** pacing — plus **$163 million in co-investments** executed during 2025. Favored sectors per Townsend Group include data centers, built-to-rent, manufactured housing, and senior housing; the fund is underweight at 6.7% versus a 7.0% real estate target.

**Pennsylvania SERS ($35B, PA)** advanced commitments to **Francisco Partners Fund VIII** and **Francisco Partners Agility Fund IV**, consistent with a broader tilt toward technology-focused buyout among mid-sized plans.

## Manager Hires and Mandate Changes

Manager selection activity in H1 2026 spans absolute return, fixed income, real estate equity, global equities, and infrastructure, with multiple funds issuing RFPs or completing finalists searches.

**IPERS** executed the most comprehensive manager hiring slate: **Florin Court Capital** (portable alpha/cat bond), **Capital Fund Management SA (CFM ISTEC Program)**, and **AHL Partners LLP (AHL Cat Bond Program)** were all approved for absolute return/portable alpha mandates, subject to due diligence. Simultaneously, **BentallGreenOak (BGO)** was hired for U.S. Core Plus real estate equity and **CBRE Group** for U.S. Core real estate equity, replacing the gap created by the elimination of the Global Smart Beta sleeve.

**LACERS ($25B, CA)** renewed one-year contracts with **Granahan Investment Management** (Small Cap Growth, ~$160.8M, 70 bps fee) and **Copeland Capital Management** (Small Cap Core, ~$297.3M, 46 bps fee), despite both managers underperforming benchmarks across nearly all periods as of December 31, 2025. Copeland has been on watch since March 2025 after posting -1.04% versus a 12.81% benchmark for FY2025. Staff and **NEPC** recommended one-year renewals to allow additional observation. The fund also approved a 3-year renewal with **Dimensional Fund Advisors** for Active Non-U.S. Emerging Markets Value Equities, and issued an **$800 million RFP for Global Listed Infrastructure** (active and passive), with proposals due June 4, 2026.

**Sacramento County Employees' Retirement System ($12B, CA)** approved a **$450 million investment with Man Numeric** for a Global Core ex-U.S. Strategy, representing a significant single-manager equity commitment.

**Colorado PERA ($60B, CO)** advanced a **Private Equity Incentive Phase-in** proposal that would raise internal manager excess return targets from 50 bps to 70 bps (1-year), 30 bps to 40 bps (3-year), and 25 bps to 30 bps (5-year) over five years — a structural tightening that reflects Aon's finding that internal management costs approximately **6.6 basis points** versus an estimated **$111 million** to externally manage equivalent assets.

**LACERA's** passive cash overlay manager search neared completion, with five finalists in contract development: **Legal & General Asset Management**, **NISA Investment Advisors**, **Parametric Portfolio Associates**, **Russell Investments**, and **State Street Investment Management**.

## Portfolio Strategy Shifts: Allocation Reviews, Benchmark Changes, and Policy Overhauls

Several plans undertook structural allocation changes in the first half of 2026, driven by asset-liability reviews, capital market assumption updates, and active risk reassessments.

**IPERS** adopted **Portfolio C**, eliminating its 5% Global Smart Beta allocation and redistributing 2% to core fixed income and 3% to public credit. The move was prompted by a tracking error of just **23 basis points** against a **150 bps target** — a concern raised directly by CEO Greg Samorajski, who flagged the fund as carrying meaningfully less active risk than its stated policy. A multi-meeting active risk review was proposed. The fund's private credit and real assets allocations both remain **below policy targets**, supporting the aggressive 2026 pacing plan.

**Colorado PERA** established a **Private Credit asset class in January 2025** (carved out from the Opportunistic bucket) and set an **Alternatives target of 6% of total fund**. The Investment Committee also identified five real estate leverage compliance exceptions during 2025, with one directly owned property reaching **76.16% LTV** against a 50% limit, necessitating reclassification from Core to Opportunistic. An amended CAP Statement of Investment Policy was adopted unanimously.

**LACERA's** 2024 Strategic Asset Allocation elevated the **Risk Reduction and Mitigation (RRM) portfolio target from 19% to 24%**, with the total RRM market value reaching **$22.26 billion** as of February 28, 2026 — providing 37 months of benefit payment liquidity. The hedge fund benchmark within RRM was revised downward from T-Bills + 250 bps to T-Bills + 200 bps in July 2024. Infrastructure target was simultaneously reduced from 5% to 4%.

**OCERS** adopted **MSCI private markets benchmarks in 2025** and is exploring AI tools for fee verification and cash flow modeling. The co-investment program was expanded beyond real estate to other private market asset classes. Meketa's 2026 capital market assumptions cited 10-year expected returns of **9.0% for private equity, 7.8% for private debt, 7.5% for infrastructure**, and **6.3% for U.S. equity** — the spread continuing to justify illiquidity premiums in institutional portfolios.

**TMRS ($40B, TX)** posted **13.29% total fund return in FY2025**, beating its benchmark by **107 basis points** and adding **$5.6 billion** to the trust, while maintaining one of the lower actuarial assumed return rates in Texas at **6.75%** (versus ERS/TRS at 7.0% and TCDRS at 7.25%). The board is conducting a trustee risk appetite survey through NEPC to inform forthcoming efficient frontier and asset allocation modeling. Current targets: Public Equity 35%, Private Equity 20%, Credit 21%, Real Assets 18%, Tactical Opportunities 6%.

**MN SBI** adopted a new **Investment Policy Statement** incorporating a 2025 Asset Allocation Study advised by **Aon Investments**, **Meketa Investment Group**, and **Albourne America**, alongside a new **Personal Securities Trading Policy** requiring quarterly holdings reporting and pre-approval for staff trades.

**CalPERS ($502B, CA)** had a **Total Fund Policy Review** listed as a first reading for its March 2026 meeting, alongside an **Asset Liability Management** first reading for both Affiliate Funds and the Public Employees' Retirement Fund. A **Reference Portfolio Benchmark Recommendation** from **Wilshire Advisors** was slated for closed session action — indicating a potentially material shift in the plan's benchmark construction.

## Performance Data: Fund Returns vs. Benchmarks

CY2025 and FY2025 performance data reflects broadly strong absolute returns across equity-heavy plans, with meaningful dispersion in alternative asset classes.

**IPERS** posted a **13.75% total fund return for CY2025** against a 13.40% benchmark, with strong contributions from International Equity (33.21% vs. 32.39% benchmark) and Public Credit (10.82% vs. 10.22%). The Liability-Aware Risk Strategies (LARS) allocation was a notable drag at **-6.88% versus a 4.18% benchmark**. Over 10 years, the fund compounded at 8.98% versus an 8.92% benchmark.

**TMRS** delivered **13.29% for FY2025**, its strongest year following a mid-2021 allocation restructuring that has lifted the fund's rolling 5-year performance to the **38th percentile** of peers. GIPS verification has now been achieved for four consecutive years — the first Texas public pension to do so.

**LACERA's** Real Assets portfolio (ex-Real Estate) returned **12.1% for the 1-year period ending December 31, 2025**, outperforming its custom benchmark of 7.6% by **450 basis points**. Natural Resources were the standout at **17.8% versus a 4.1% benchmark**, driven by record gold demand and metals tied to AI/electrification themes. Infrastructure co-investments returned **23.1% since inception** (March 2023).

**LACERA's** RRM portfolio returned **6.8% for the 1-year period ending February 2026** versus a 6.0% benchmark. Long-Term Government Bonds (effective duration 13.9 years) remain deeply negative since inception at **-6.4% annualized from November 2021**, reflecting the 2022 rate spike.

**SCERS ($12B, CA)** reported an **11.1% return for FY2024-2025** against the 6.75% actuarial assumption, driving a **$672.7 million reduction in Net Pension Liability** to $1.019 billion.

**OCERS** reported a total fund return of **11.3% YTD through mid-November 2025** against a 7.0% benchmark.

**Colorado PERA's CAP funds** showed mixed Q4 2025 results: **U.S. SMID Cap** outperformed at 1.7% vs. 1.1%, while the **International Stock Fund** lagged at 3.6% vs. 5.1%.

**LACERS small cap managers** produced deeply negative Q4 2025 results — **Granahan Investment Management** returned **-10.18% versus a 1.22% benchmark** in Q4 alone, with FY2025 performance of 2.38% versus 13.01%. **Copeland Capital Management** posted **-1.04% for FY2025 versus a 12.81% benchmark**, underscoring the watch status assigned in March 2025.

**MN SBI's** Combined Funds ranked top quartile or better versus public pension peers across all measured time periods (1, 3, 5, 10, and 20 years) in TUCS, with a 10-year return of **9.9% versus a 9.7% benchmark** through December 31, 2025.

## Governance, ESG, and Leadership Transitions

Governance activity in H1 2026 is notably elevated, with leadership changes, legislative engagement, technology adoption, and politically sensitive divestment mandates all appearing across multiple boards.

**Leadership transitions** were concentrated in California. **LACERA** saw the retirement of several senior executives in March 2026 — Assistant Executive Officer Laura Guglielmo, Chief Counsel Vincent Lim, Division Manager Bernie Buenaflor, and Assistant Division Manager Leilani Ignacio — alongside the appointment of **Leisha Collins as Chief Internal Audit** (8-0 dual vote of the Board of Retirement and Board of Investments). **IPERS Deputy CEO Rick Hindman** retired in January 2026. **TMRS Director of Actuarial Services Leslee Hardy** retired April 30, 2026 after 18 years. **LACERS actuary Andy Yeung** retired February 21, 2026, replaced by **Emily Klare**.

**LACERA** approved a new **three-firm fiduciary counsel panel** — **Klausner Kaufman Jensen & Levinson** ($500/hour), **Nossaman LLP** ($600–$720/hour), and **Reed Smith LLP** — for a five-year term, following an RFP in which seven firms submitted proposals and four were interviewed.

**AI adoption** emerged as a cross-cutting theme. LACERA formally adopted an **AI Policy** (approved 7-0 at the March 4 board meeting), with a public summary requiring human oversight for all final decisions and prohibiting sensitive data in unapproved tools. Active projects include a lacera.gov chatbot, AI-assisted disability retirement report organization, and AI-enhanced Excel tools for investment analysis. OCERS disclosed AI implementation for fee verification and cash flow modeling. Colorado PERA identified AI in investments as a priority 2026 trustee education topic.

**ESG and divestment** issues generated divergent board responses. **Colorado PERA** included **divestment from companies with prohibitions against Israel** as a consent agenda action item, maintaining a Restricted Company List consistent with statutory obligations. **LACERA** submitted a comment letter to the UK Financial Conduct Authority regarding proposed sustainability reporting requirements. **Charterhouse Capital Partners** achieved **B Corp certification in May 2025**, with net-zero-by-2030 targets embedded in CCP XII's marketing to WSIB. The **Private Equity Stakeholder Project** submitted public comment at OCERS regarding Thoma Bravo's ownership of RealPage, illustrating continued activist monitoring of PE-owned portfolio companies within pension portfolios.

**Funded status improvements** were broadly reported. **IPERS'** funded status rose from 90.75% to **92.17%** per the 2025 actuarial valuation, with the UAL declining from $4.38 billion to **$3.84 billion**. **SCERS** reported a Net Pension Liability decline of $672.7 million. **LACERA's** UAAL stood at **14.63%** with a $607 million Star Reserve and a 7.00% assumed return. **Dallas P&F ($3B, TX)** approved a revised January 1, 2025 actuarial valuation by **Segal** incorporating its **December 2025 Settlement Agreement**, which eliminated COLA provisions — a structurally significant benefit modification approved 9-1.

**Legislative engagement** was active at the state level. **LACERA** tracked **AB 1619** (proposed increase in board meeting compensation from $100 to $320, unchanged since 1982) and supported AB 2780 (omnibus CERL technical amendments), adopting a "Support If Amended" position on the former. **California SB 827** added a two-hour fiscal and financial training requirement for county retirement board members effective January 1, 2026, prompting **SCERS** to amend its Board Education Policy. **TMRS** began planning for the **2027 Texas Legislative Session**, identifying a Director of Governmental Relations hire as a critical near-term priority. **Colorado PERA** received public testimony supporting **House Bill 26-1026** during its March legislative update.
