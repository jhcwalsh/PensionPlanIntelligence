## 1. Private Market Deployment Remains Broad-Based

Private market commitment activity was sustained across all four weeks of May, spanning buyout, growth equity, venture capital, private credit, and real assets. **LACERA** (~$75B) was among the most active deployers: its CIO approved **$100 million** to **Dragoneer Select Opportunities Fund, L.P.**, **$125 million** as a secondary investment in **IDR Core Property Trust**, and **$200 million** to **Principal Enhanced Property Fund, L.P.** during executive session on May 13 (doc_id=4079). Earlier in the month, LACERA confirmed a **$190 million** commitment to **Bregal Sagemount V, L.P.** (doc_id=3474). **LACERS** (~$25B) considered commitments across eleven funds spanning venture capital, private equity, and private credit totaling over **$335 million**, including up to **$80 million** to **Hayfin Tactical Solutions Fund (US Parallel) LP**, up to **$50 million** to **Francisco Partners VIII, L.P.**, and up to **$40 million** to **New Enterprise Associates 19, L.P.** (doc_id=4210).

**TRSL** (~$25B) had previously approved potential commitments of up to **$150 million** combined to **Centerbridge Special Credit Partners V, L.P.** and **Centerbridge Keystone Fund, L.P.**, up to **$100 million** combined to **Francisco Partners VIII, L.P.** and **Francisco Partners Agility IV, L.P.**, up to **$75 million** to **LS Power Fund VI, L.P.**, and up to **$25 million** to **Energy Capital Partners VI, L.P.** (doc_id=3605). **KPPA** (~$20B) approved an additional **$100 million** commitment to **ITE Rail Fund, L.P.** as part of its Real Return allocation, following full deployment of an initial **$100 million** commitment by October 2025 (doc_id=4207). **MainePERS** (~$18B) approved commitments of up to **$15 million** each to **General Catalyst Group XIII** and **GC Creation Fund III** (doc_id=4008), while noting that **Alternative Credit** stands significantly below its 15% policy target at **7.9% of fund** and **Private Equity** is above its 10% strategic target at **15.1%** (doc_id=4006).

## 2. Manager Governance: Hires, Renewals, and Consultant Scrutiny

Manager selection and consultant governance were recurring themes throughout May. **CCPF** (~$12B) approved **Driehaus Capital Management LLC** and **Causeway Capital Management LLC** for international small cap equity mandates of up to **$225 million** each, by a **7-2** vote, following an RFP process in which 37 firms responded and three were named finalists (doc_id=3706). **CTPF** (~$18B) voted **8-0-1** to approve RFPs for International Small Cap Growth and Domestic Small Cap Value managers after first-look presentations from **Conestoga Capital Advisors**, **Phocas Financial Corporation**, and **William Blair** (doc_id=3974).

CTPF's consultant situation drew particular attention: the board voted **9-1** to extend **Callan's** investment consulting contract for one year and **1-9** against proceeding with **Cerity Partners** as the new investment consultant, citing concerns about private equity ownership and conflicts of interest; a new RFP was approved **10-1** (doc_id=3973). **LACERA** extended **StepStone Group LP** as its private equity investment consulting services agreement for one year by a **6-0** vote (doc_id=3474). **SMCERA** (~$5B) engaged **Meketa Investment Group** as new investment consultant following a competitive RFP process in December 2025, with FY2026-27 fees budgeted at **$675,000** (doc_id=4157).

On the manager side, **LACERS** approved a **3-year** contract renewal with **Loomis, Sayles & Company L.P.** for active High Yield Fixed Income on a **$416 million** allocation by a **6-0** vote, and a **1-year** renewal with **Informed Momentum Company LLC** for active U.S. Small Cap Growth Equities (**$132 million**) despite the manager being on watch status for underperformance, also by a **6-0** vote (doc_id=4210). **TRS-OK** (~$19B) placed potential terminations of **Frontier Capital**, **Geneva Capital**, and **Northern Trust** index accounts on its agenda as part of a domestic equity portfolio structure review (doc_id=4172).

*Sources: [CCPF — Minutes — April 02, 2026](?doc=3706), [CTPF — Minutes — April 09, 2026](?doc=3974), [CTPF — Minutes — April 09, 2026](?doc=3973), [LACERA — Agenda — May 13, 2026](?doc=3474), [SMCERA — Board Pack — May 21, 2026](?doc=4157), [LACERS — Agenda — May 26, 2026](?doc=4210), [TRS-OK — Agenda — May 20, 2026](?doc=4172)*

**Practical implication:** Consultant ownership structures are emerging as a board-level governance screen; plans conducting consultant reviews should document conflict-of-interest analysis explicitly in board materials.

## 3. Performance Divergence: Private Markets Lag Public Benchmarks at Several Plans

Performance data across May revealed notable divergence between public and private market returns. **TRS Texas** (~$200B, valued at **$235.2 billion as of December 31, 2025**) posted a 1-year return of **15.9%** versus a **14.1%** benchmark and a 3-year return of **11.1%** versus **9.0%** through December 31, 2025, ranking in the top quartile among peers for QTD and 1-year periods (doc_id=3998). Commodities returned **102.6%** and Absolute Return **18.4%** for FY2025; Real Estate was the notable laggard at **-3.5%** versus a **3.2%** benchmark (doc_id=3998).

**LACERA** reported a total fund AUM of **$89.6 billion** as of March 2026, with a 1-year net return of **11.8%** against a **13.4%** policy benchmark, and a FYTD return of **6.5%** versus a **9.0%** benchmark (doc_id=3474). **PERS-OR** (~$110B) reported a 1-year return of **8.40%** versus a policy benchmark of **13.10%** for the period ending March 2026, with total OPERF Regular Account NAV of approximately **$101.4 billion**; Private Equity returned **5.41%** against a **24.43%** benchmark over the same period (doc_id=4081). **CalPERS** (~$502B) reported its Infrastructure Portfolio generated a 1-year net return of **5.1%** versus a **3.2%** benchmark for the period ending December 31, 2025, with portfolio NAV of **$23.2 billion** (doc_id=3956). CalPERS' Private Debt Portfolio returned **12.4%** versus a **9.2%** benchmark for the 1-year period, with NAV of **$24.2 billion** representing **4.0%** of the total fund against an **8%** long-term target (doc_id=3954).

The **NYC Teachers' Retirement System** reported preliminary total plan returns of **1.5%** month-to-date, **4.9%** calendar year-to-date, and **11.9%** fiscal year-to-date as of May 11, 2026, with Emerging Markets Equities leading at **43.0%** FYTD (doc_id=4057).

## 4. Actuarial Assumptions Under Active Review; Funded Status Pressures Persist

Actuarial assumption reviews were a consistent theme across three weeks of May. **LAFPP** (~$29B) is scheduled to consider reducing its investment return assumption from **7.00% to 6.75%** per annum, with a **55% confidence level** cited in the actuarial recommendation; the City employer contribution to LAFPP increased by **$42.2 million (6.6%)** due to revised salary figures and Measure FF transfers (doc_id=4134). **ERSRI** (~$10B) considered its 2026 Actuarial Experience Study, with **GRS** recommending no change to the **7.0%** investment return assumption and no change to the **2.50%** inflation assumption; the Teachers funded ratio stands at **67.9%** and State Employees at **63.9%** (doc_id=4229). **TRS Texas** unanimously adopted the 2026 Actuarial Experience Study confirming a **7%** investment return assumption (doc_id=4151). **MainePERS** adopted actuarial assumptions recommended by **Cheiron** from the PLD Consolidated Plan experience study (doc_id=4008).

**DPFP** (~$3B) remains severely underfunded at a **39.08%** funded ratio on a market value basis as of January 1, 2026, with a total actuarial accrued liability of **$5.76 billion** against market value assets of **$2.25 billion** and projected full funding not until **2053** (doc_id=4060). The City's FY2027 ADC is **$286.0 million**, while the Funding Agreement estimated contribution is **$247.0 million** (doc_id=4061). DPFP posted a **15.49%** market value return for FY2025 versus a **6.50%** assumed rate (doc_id=4062).

*Sources: [LAFPP — Agenda — May 21, 2026](?doc=4134), [ERSRI — Board Pack — May 20, 2026](?doc=4229), [TRS Texas — Board Pack — May 01, 2026](?doc=4151), [MainePERS — Agenda — May 14, 2026](?doc=4008), [DPFP — Board Pack — May 08, 2026](?doc=4060), [DPFP — Agenda — May 08, 2026](?doc=4061), [DPFP — Agenda — May 08, 2026](?doc=4062)*

## 5. CIO Transitions and Legislative Disclosure Risk

Leadership transitions and legislative risk to alternative investment disclosure were concurrent governance themes in May. **LACERS** is managing a CIO transition following CIO **Rod June's** announced retirement effective July 2026, with board recruitment pending Mayoral and City Council approval of a civil service exemption re-authorization (doc_id=3971). **ERS-HI** (~$23B) held a special meeting on April 24, 2026, selecting a candidate for an interim CIO position effective June 1, 2026, following the departure of CIO **Kristin Varela**, and approved issuing an RFQ for an executive search firm to recruit a permanent CIO (doc_id=3613).

On the legislative front, **OCERS** (~$25B) adopted an Oppose position on **SB 1319 (Durazo)**, a California bill that would expand CPRA disclosure requirements for public pension funds' alternative investments, and authorized the CEO to transmit an opposition letter (doc_id=3972). **CalPERS** (~$502B) also had SB 1319 as an action item on its May 18, 2026 board agenda (doc_id=3953).

*Sources: [LACERS — Agenda — May 12, 2026](?doc=3971), [ERS-HI — Minutes — April 24, 2026](?doc=3613), [OCERS — Agenda — May 18, 2026](?doc=3972), [CalPERS — Board Pack — May 08, 2026](?doc=3953)*

**Practical implication:** California-domiciled plans with significant alternative investment portfolios should monitor SB 1319's legislative progress closely, as expanded CPRA disclosure obligations could affect manager negotiating dynamics and fund access terms.
