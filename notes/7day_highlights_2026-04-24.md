## Investment Consultant Transitions

Two plans formalized consultant changes this period. **Oklahoma Public Employees Retirement System** (**OPERS-OK**, $10B) approved the transition of investment consulting services from **Verus Advisory, Inc.** to **Cerity Partners LLC** as part of a broader FY2027 contract renewal cycle that also renewed 13 manager agreements spanning domestic equity, fixed income, and index strategies — including **Baillie Gifford**, **BlackRock**, **Hoisington Investment Management**, **TCW Metropolitan West**, and **Westfield Capital Management**.

Separately, **Chicago Teachers Pension Fund** (**CTPF**, $18B) voted 6-4 in March to hire **Verus** as its investment consultant for a five-year contract beginning FY2027, replacing **Callan LLC** after a 20-year relationship. Illinois Pension Code caps consulting contracts at five years. The irony of Verus simultaneously winning CTPF and losing OPERS-OK underscores the competitive consultant market. **Teacher Retirement System of Texas** (**TRS Texas**, $200B) also has a special trustee committee (Allred, Ball, Elliott) tasked with recommending a Board investment consultant at the April 2026 meeting — that recommendation is now imminent.

## Climate Reporting and ESG Strategy

**NYC Retirement Systems / TRS** (**NYC Retirement**, $260B) presented its FY2025 Annual Climate Report at the April 16 investment meeting. Scope 1 and 2 financed emissions intensity fell **49%** since the December 2019 baseline, surpassing the 32% interim target. Climate solution investments reached **$7.34 billion** against a 2035 goal of **$19 billion**. However, the science-based target coverage goal of 70% of financed emissions was missed — only 27% of financed emissions are covered by validated targets as of June 30, 2025. TRS declined to set formal Scope 3 interim reduction targets, citing data quality and double-counting concerns, and adopted the **Net Zero Investment Framework (NZIF 2.0)** for listed equity and corporate fixed income. Nvidia, Microsoft, and Amazon together comprise approximately 30% of TRS's total climate solutions portfolio.

## Governance and Asset Reclassification

**LACERA** ($75B) advanced a significant accounting change at its April 20 Joint Organizational Governance Committee meeting: reclassifying its **Gateway Plaza** headquarters from an investment asset to a capital asset. The recommended Option 4 — treating the title holding company as a blended component unit in Year 1 (effective July 1, 2026) and dissolving it in Year 2 — will produce an estimated **$30 million non-cash write-down** and an approximately **8 basis point** drag on reported investment performance. The move aligns with Board-approved policy to reduce separate account real estate in favor of commingled funds. New external auditor **CliftonLarsonAllen LLP** reviewed the options and raised no objections. The CERL 21 basis point administrative expense cap was a key constraint in evaluating all options.

*Sources: [LACERA — Agenda — April 20, 2026](?doc=2008)*

## Manager Hires and Mandate Changes

**LAFPP** ($29B) approved a three-year contract extension with **MacKay Shields LLC** for high yield fixed income management through June 30, 2029. MacKay Shields manages **$771.9 million** for the fund (up from $668.9 million at the July 2023 contract start) at approximately **33 basis points** — 23rd percentile on fees. The manager was placed on watch as of December 31, 2025 due to below-median 3-year peer performance; its 1-year return of 7.59% trailed the benchmark's 8.5%, though 5-, 7-, and 10-year results are above benchmark. Consultant **RVK, Inc.** concurred with the extension recommendation.

Earlier in the period, LAFPP's February activity included awarding a three-year contract to **Acadian Asset Management LLC** for a **$225 million** Active International Small Capitalization Equity mandate (5-0 vote), terminating six managers as part of its U.S. Equity Structure Study — including **Northern Trust Investments**, **AllianceBernstein**, **Channing Capital Management**, **Westwood Management**, and **Palisade Capital Management** — and launching a search for a new Small Cap Value manager.
